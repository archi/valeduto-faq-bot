keywords: manual, install
title: Manually installing/upgrading/downgrading Valetudo
short-title: manual install
text:
It may happen that you forgot to package valetudo when building the rooted firmware, or Valetudo was lost in a reset, or you want to install a different version of Valetudo.
These are the manual steps to do so.

1. Connect to your robot over ssh
2. Check the architecture of your robot: <pre>uname -a</pre>. Look for something like armv7 or aarch64
3. Find the location of valetudo. Old Roborocks (V1, S5) used <pre>/usr/local/bin</pre>, newer Roborocks use <pre>/mnt/data</pre>, Dreame uses <pre>/data</pre> and Viomi is <pre>/mnt/UDISK</pre>
4. Kill valetudo: <pre>killall valetudo</pre>
5. Delete the old binary (replace folder with what you found in 3): <pre>rm /data/valetudo</pre>
    or make a backup: <pre>mv /data/valetudo /data/valetudo.old</pre>
    (if you get an error, you did not kill valetudo, see step 4)
6. download the latest binary, choice depends on step 2, folder depends on step 3: <pre>wget https://github.com/Hypfer/Valetudo/releases/latest/download/valetudo-{armv7,armv7-lowmem,aarch64} -O /data/valetudo</pre>
7. Make it executable: <pre>chmod +x /data/valetudo</pre>
8. Reboot your robot: <pre>reboot</pre>
9. Wait, it will be minutes before your map shows up again
